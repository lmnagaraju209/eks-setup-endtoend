# Cost Comparison: US vs Indian Development

I get asked about this constantly. Let me break down the real numbers and what you're actually getting.

---

## The Numbers

### Indian Development Team

**Hourly Rates**:
- Senior Kubernetes/AWS engineer: $30-$60/hour
- Mid-level developer: $20-$40/hour  
- Junior developer: $10-$25/hour

**For this project** (2-3 developers):
- **Direct cost**: $20,000-$40,000
- **Timeline**: 6-8 weeks (often longer)
- **Team composition**: Usually 1 senior + 1-2 mid-level

### US-Based (Me)

**Hourly Rate**: $150-$200/hour

**For this project**:
- **Direct cost**: $45,000-$55,000 (fixed price)
- **Timeline**: 4-5 weeks

---

## The Real Cost Comparison

### Upfront Savings with Indian Team
**$10,000-$25,000 cheaper** on paper.

### Hidden Costs (Indian Team)

**Time zone overhead**: 10-12 hour difference
- Meetings at odd hours (your 9am is their 9pm)
- Async communication slows everything down
- What takes 1 day with me = 2-3 days with offshore
- **Cost impact**: +20-30% time overhead

**Communication barriers**:
- Language differences (even if they speak English)
- Cultural context gaps
- "Let me check with the team" delays
- Misunderstandings that require rework
- **Cost impact**: +15-25% rework

**Quality and experience**:
- Finding the right team takes time
- Less experience with EKS specifically
- More learning curve = more mistakes
- Quality issues that show up later
- **Cost impact**: +10-20% fixes/redos

**Project management**:
- You'll spend more time managing them
- Daily standups across time zones
- Status updates, progress tracking
- **Cost impact**: Your time (hard to quantify, but real)

**Timeline delays**:
- 6-8 weeks instead of 4-5
- Delays cost money (opportunity cost)
- **Cost impact**: Depends on your situation

### Total Real Cost (Indian Team)

**Best case**: $25,000-$35,000 (if everything goes smoothly)  
**Realistic**: $30,000-$50,000 (with overhead and rework)  
**Worst case**: $40,000-$60,000+ (if things go sideways)

**Timeline**: 6-8 weeks (often 8-10)

### My Cost

**Fixed**: $45,000-$55,000  
**Timeline**: 4-5 weeks (guaranteed)

## When Indian Development Makes Sense

**Good fit if**:
- You have a project manager to handle coordination
- Timeline isn't critical (can wait 8-10 weeks)
- You're comfortable with async communication
- You've worked with the team before
- Budget is the primary constraint
- You have time to manage quality

**Not a good fit if**:
- You need it done fast (4-5 weeks)
- You want to be hands-off
- Communication is critical
- You need deep EKS/AWS expertise
- You want fixed price/certainty
- You've had bad offshore experiences before

## My Honest Take

I've fixed enough offshore projects to know the pattern. The ones that work well:
- Have an experienced project manager
- Give the team clear requirements
- Have time for back-and-forth
- Don't need it done fast
- Are comfortable with the process

The ones that don't:
- Rush the timeline
- Unclear requirements
- Communication issues
- Quality problems show up later
- End up costing more than US-based

**If you're considering Indian developers**, make sure you:
1. Have a good project manager
2. Can handle 6-8 week timeline
3. Are comfortable with time zone differences
4. Have clear, detailed requirements
5. Budget for 20-30% overhead/rework

**If you want it done right, fast, with less headache**, the US-based option is worth the extra cost.

## Hybrid Option

If budget is tight, we could do:
- **Phase 1 (Infrastructure)**: With me ($7,500-$9,000) - critical, needs expertise
- **Phase 2 (Application)**: With Indian team ($4,000-$6,000) - more straightforward
- **Phase 3-5**: Back with me for CI/CD and GitOps

This gets you the critical expertise where it matters, saves money on the more routine work.

**Indian team**: Cheaper upfront, longer timeline, more management, variable quality

The "cheaper" option often costs more in the long run. But I get it - sometimes budget is the constraint. If that's the case, let's talk about phasing or reducing scope rather than going offshore.
